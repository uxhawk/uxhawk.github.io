Usability Testing For An Android Beta App

Summary

Project Directive

In under 100 days, deliver a beta application to the Google Play Store. The application should:

  1. Educate users with information about vehicle care.
  2. Give users the ability to track their vehicle's health statistics.
  3. Demonstrate a new interaction model from a trusted source via engaging content and cutting edge design.

My Contributions

As the lead UX designer I on the project:

  1. Conducted user research and usability testing
  2. Defined app IA
  3. Built prototypes and wireframes to share concepts with UI designers and developers
  4. Prioritized features and the post-launch road map
Developing Personas

Target Audience

Ranging from the new car owner, to the experienced mechanic, this app app had a wide audience. I researched and designed several personas of with varying levels of car knowledge and repair skills to prioritize features for the app.

Wireframes

Early Concepts

From customer interviews, I knew the interface needed to seem playful and game-like to achieve our goal for the interaction model: 3-5 visits per week. Nothing resonated that fun sentiment more than a retro 8-bit-style UI.

I propsed a composite health score that users maintained by completing maintenance tasks and challenges. I gave the app tabs so users could switch between summary views and challenges.

App Walkthrough

Introducing Features

Usability testing content helped the team arrive at the perfect length for a new user tutorial. An optional four slide tutorial introduced users to the app's 8-bit style and other key features.
