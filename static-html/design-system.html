Design System

Project Directive

A Swedish golf company initiated a site redesign during the winter offseason. As you can imagine, there is not a lot of golf happening from December to March in Sweden, so it was the perfect time to update styles and task flows with new content.

I knew the ideal tool to guide the redesign was a design system focused on Atomic Design principles. Rather than picking and choosing task flows or components to update, a system built from basic components (atoms) would create the most effective technique to share styles with developers and review updates with client stakeholders.

Like any application, design systems must be easy to navigate. To that end, I compared popular design systems like Material and Mailchimp to identify best practices for information architecture and to spot slick design trends. The result, was a system of atoms, organisms, and molecules that fed into templates and pages with excellent usability and aesthetic, modern appeal.
